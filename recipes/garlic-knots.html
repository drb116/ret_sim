Garlic Knots

Ingredients

  • ¾ cup warm water (about 110°F)
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 2¼ teaspoons (1 packet) active dry yeast
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (plus more for dusting)
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ¼ cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley
  • ½ teaspoon Italian seasoning
  • ½ teaspoon garlic powder (optional, for extra flavor)
  • Grated Parmesan cheese, for topping (optional)

Directions

  1. In a small bowl, combine warm water, sugar, and yeast. Stir gently and let sit for 5–10 minutes, until foamy.
  2. Add olive oil, flour, and salt. Mix until a soft dough forms, then knead for about 5 minutes until smooth and elastic. Add flour as needed if dough is sticky.
  3. Place dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover, and let rise in a warm spot until doubled, about 1 hour.
  4. Punch down dough and divide into 10–12 equal pieces. Roll each piece into a rope (about 6 inches long) and tie into a knot. Place knots on a parchment-lined baking sheet.
  5. Cover loosely and let rise 20–30 minutes while preheating the oven to 400°F.
  6. Bake for 12–15 minutes, or until lightly golden brown.
  7. Meanwhile, melt butter in a small saucepan and stir in minced garlic, parsley, and Italian seasoning. Brush over warm knots right after baking. Sprinkle with Parmesan if desired.
