The Best Dinner Rolls

Ingredients

  • 3/4 cup warm milk (110°F)
  • 1 tablespoon active dry yeast
  • 3 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • ½ large egg
  • 2½ tablespoons unsalted butter, softened
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 2 to 2½ cups all-purpose flour
  • Additional melted butter for brushing

Directions

  1. In a small bowl, combine warm milk, yeast, and a pinch of sugar. Let stand for 5 to 10 minutes until foamy.
  2. In a large bowl or mixer, add sugar, egg, butter, and salt. Mix until smooth. Add the yeast mixture and stir to combine.
  3. Gradually add flour, one cup at a time, until a soft dough forms. Knead 6 to 8 minutes, until smooth and elastic.
  4. Place dough in a greased bowl, cover, and let rise in a warm place until doubled, about 1 hour.
  5. Punch down the dough and divide into 12–15 equal portions. Shape into balls and place in a greased 9×13-inch pan.
  6. Cover and let rise again until doubled, 30 to 45 minutes.
  7. Bake at 375°F for 15–18 minutes, until golden brown. Brush warm rolls with melted butter before serving.

Comments

This is half the recipe. I make it into anywhere from 8 to 12 rolls. The second rise often can take as much as 2 hours. Do not cook on an aluminum pan.
