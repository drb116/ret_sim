One-Pan Cheeseburger Rice

Ingredients

  • Yield: 4 servings
  • 1 pound ground turkey or ground beef
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • ½ teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder, divided
  • 3 tablespoons ketchup
  • 2½ tablespoons Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 (14.5-ounce) can beef broth
  • ¼ cup water
  • 1 cup white rice
  • 3 tablespoons mayonnaise
  • 1 teaspoon white truffle oil
  • 8 ounces cheddar cheese, shredded and divided
  • Optional toppings: chopped dill pickles, shredded lettuce

Instructions

  1. Step 1: Preheat oven to low broil.
  2. Step 2: Heat a large oven-safe skillet over medium-high heat. Add ground beef or turkey and cook, breaking it apart, until browned, about 7 minutes. Drain excess fat.
  3. Step 3: Season with salt, pepper, ½ tsp garlic powder, ketchup, and Worcestershire sauce. Stir to combine.
  4. Step 4: Add beef broth, water, and rice. Bring to a boil, stirring to loosen browned bits from the pan.
  5. Step 5: Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer until rice is tender and liquid is absorbed, about 18 to 20 minutes.
  6. Step 6: Stir together mayonnaise, ½ tsp garlic powder, and truff oil in a small bowl to make the burger sauce.
  7. Step 7: Stir half of the shredded cheese into the rice mixture. Sprinkle remaining cheese over the top.
  8. Step 8: Place skillet under the broiler until cheese is melted and bubbly, about 1 to 2 minutes.
  9. Step 9: Serve topped with burger sauce, pickles, and shredded lettuce if desired.

Comments

Original recipe uses a different burger sauce with a little ketchup and mustard mixed in to the mayo. This would be a good substitue if you didn't have truffule oil.
